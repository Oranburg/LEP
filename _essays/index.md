# Essays and Working Papers

These pieces develop the intellectual identity of the Law and Entrepreneurship Program: the doctrinal lineage that supports it, the institutional setting it grows in, and the cluster of arguments about formation, work, and the firm that gives the Program its shape.

Items marked *Pre-release* are working drafts. They render on this site but their bodies sit behind a courtesy access key. If you would like a key, email Seth at <a href="mailto:oranburg@cua.edu">oranburg@cua.edu</a>.

## Working papers

### [Three States of a Living Tradition](/LEP/essays/three-states/)
*Pre-release.* Athens, Jerusalem, Rome as three different institutional answers to the same question: how does a tradition stay alive when the conditions that produced it change? An argument about what a university owes its founding tradition and what the tradition owes the university.

### [The Deskilling Loop](/LEP/essays/deskilling-loop/)
*Pre-release.* Documentation systems built to standardize professional judgment end up replacing it. The clinician charts more than she treats; the lawyer drafts more than she counsels. A diagnosis, then a proposal for what professional formation has to defend.

### [AI as Havruta](/LEP/essays/ai-as-havruta/)
