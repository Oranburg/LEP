# Private-Law Faculty and Curricular Expansion

**The course spectrum the program needs, and the faculty to teach it.**

Private law is the doctrine entrepreneurial students go on to practice: contracts, business associations, securities, commercial law. The program needs more than its current course set to serve student demand, and it needs faculty who can teach transactional practice at the depth that demand requires.

## Why now

The path from doctrine to practice is not yet complete. At least one more clinical course and one more experiential course are needed to round it out. The hiring and the curricular design are the same conversation, and it is the right year to have it.

## The plan

- Map the full course spectrum to offer, across four kinds: podium, experiential, clinical, and seminar.
- Identify the faculty each kind of course requires, and recruit at least one additional private-law teacher who can carry a clinical or experiential course.
- Read student demand as the driver of what comes first.
- Plan for a clinical-director role as the clinic grows, a faculty member who manages clinical professors rather than only teaching clinic students. The program will need it once the clinical offering expands.

## The board ask

Endorse curricular expansion as a multi-year priority and support the case for additional private-law faculty lines. Candid reactions on the course spectrum, on student demand, and on the clinical-director path will shape the plan.
