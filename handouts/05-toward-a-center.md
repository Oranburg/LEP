# Toward a Catholic Business Law Center

**A five-year ambition to test against the board's judgment.**

The program's growth path points toward a named center for Catholic business law: an institutional home for fellowships, scholarship, and convening that reaches beyond entrepreneurship into the adjacent fields of investment and nonprofit governance.

## Why now

The engine of growth is already visible. Fellowships for students and for future faculty, building on a model already proven within the school, are how the program scales its people. As the subject matter broadens past entrepreneurship, a center becomes the natural form, and a name becomes something the board and the University can rally behind. Naming the ambition early shapes the fellowships and the fundraising that would make it real.

## The plan

- Grow through fellowships for students and future faculty.
- Extend the subject matter to investment law and nonprofit governance alongside entrepreneurship.
- Frame a five-year path toward a named Catholic business law center.

## The board ask

Treat the center as a long-horizon ambition, and tell us whether it is one the board wants to help build. Early endorsement, more than early funding, is what the next steps need.
