# Entrepreneurship: Curing Poverty

**This year's program theme, and an invitation to shape the years that follow.**

Each year the program chooses one topic in entrepreneurship and law within the Catholic intellectual tradition, and builds two or three main events plus supporting activities around it. The topic for AY 2026-27 is *Entrepreneurship: Curing Poverty*.

## Why it matters

The theme dignifies the entrepreneurial lawyer as a co-creator within the Catholic intellectual tradition. It gives the year focus, and it gives the program a public intellectual identity that a board member can describe in a sentence. A single topic per year, returned to from several angles, is how the program builds a body of work rather than a series of unrelated events.

## The plan

- Convene two or three flagship events on the theme, with ancillary activities across the year.
- Anchor the scholarly convening, the *Law and New Things* symposium, inside the theme.
- Choose each successive year's topic deliberately, so the themes accumulate into a series.

## The board ask

Respond to this year's topic, and propose topics for the years that follow. The board's judgment on what resonates with the University's mission and with its own networks is exactly the input the series needs.
