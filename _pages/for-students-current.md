# For current CUA students

You are at CUA Law and want to know what to do. This page is action-oriented. Find your year, take the next step.

## If you are a 1L (Spring 2026)

1. **Email the program directors** at hoffarth@cua.edu to be added to the cohort mailing list and notified about the first cohort coffee in September 2026.
2. **Register for the Business Boot Camp** when it appears in the Fall 2026 1L registration menu. One credit, pass-fail. Designed for students with no prior business background.
3. **Attend at least one public lecture** from the program's event series before deciding whether to commit.
4. **Read one entry-point essay** from the [readings page]({{ '/readings/' | relative_url }}) before 2L registration opens.
5. **Plan your 2L electives.** Recommended core: Business Associations and Federal Income Tax in 2L fall; Securities Regulation in 2L spring.

If you have not taken a business or economics course before, you are not behind. The Boot Camp exists specifically for you.

## If you are a 2L

1. **Apply for the LEP Clinic** through CUA Law's clinical-program application for the spring or fall semester. The clinic is the operational center of the program; students counsel real client ventures and graduate with a redacted document portfolio.
2. **Request a practitioner mentorship pairing** by emailing the directors. Mentor pairings are being made for AY 2026-27. Tell the directors what kind of practice you are exploring (transactional, regulatory, entrepreneurial, in-house, fintech, public-interest) and what kind of mentor would be most useful.
3. **Attend the monthly cohort meetings.** First meeting: first Monday of September 2026.
4. **Take the recommended 2L sequence:**

| Semester | Recommended courses |
|---|---|
| 2L Fall | Business Associations (required for the track); Federal Income Tax (recommended); one elective |
| 2L Spring | Securities Regulation; Mergers and Acquisitions Survey OR enter the LEP Clinic first semester; one elective |

## If you are a 3L

1. **Apply for an externship** at a federal agency, regulatory shop, or DC firm office through the CUA Law externship office, with LEP support on practitioner contacts. Specific target categories: SEC, CFTC, FDIC, OCC, FTC, DOJ Antitrust, IRS, CFPB, FCC, congressional offices, and the DC offices of national firms.
2. **Continue the clinic** if you began it as a 2L. The 3L clinic year is where the document portfolio gets finalized.
3. **Consider an independent-study writing project** with a faculty member on a question that connects the program's intellectual frame to your career direction. A solid 3L writing project becomes a writing sample in callback interviews.
4. **Use the cohort and the alumni network** as job-search resources. Email the directors to request introductions.
5. **Recommended 3L sequence:**

| Semester | Recommended courses |
|---|---|
| 3L Fall | LEP Clinic (continuing); Banking and Financial Regulation OR Antitrust; one externship |
| 3L Spring | Transactional Practicum (when launched) OR independent study; bar-required electives; final externship or capstone |

## Across all years: what the program offers you

- **The cohort:** small annual group of students committed to the same intellectual and professional direction
- **The monthly meeting:** first Monday of each month, snacks and conversation
- **Mentorship pairings:** one faculty mentor and one practitioner mentor, minimum two meetings per semester
- **The clinic:** ABA-approved clinical course with real client ventures, the target portfolio described on the [prospective students page]({{ '/for-students/prospective/' | relative_url }})
- **The Boot Camp:** one credit pass-fail entry course, launching Fall 2026
- **The Transactional Practicum:** two credit deal simulation, launching Spring 2028
- **The lecture series:** practitioners and entrepreneurs through the academic year
- **Conference funding:** up to one thousand dollars per cohort student per year for one relevant conference
- **The intellectual frame:** the *societas* and *shutafut* tradition as the doctrinal vocabulary you use to read deal documents
