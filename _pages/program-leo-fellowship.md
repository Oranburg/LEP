# Leo Fellowship in Entrepreneurial Lawyering

A faculty-development initiative that recruits, supports, and retains private-law faculty (contracts, business associations, securities, commercial law) who can teach transactional practice at the depth entrepreneurial students need. Each program is described below in two parallel framings. The intellectual substance is identical; the vocabulary changes with the funder's preferred register.

## The problem

Private-law faculty at law schools below the top twenty are recruited and evaluated on doctrinal-publication metrics that do not reward the integration of substantive doctrine with transactional practice. The faculty who can teach contracts as the law of partnership at shared risk, who can read a SAFE through the *societas* lens of Aquinas (*Summa Theologiae* II-II q. 78 a. 2 ad 5) and the *shutafut* lens of Maimonides (*Mishneh Torah*, Hilkhot Mattenot Aniyim 10:7), and who can carry that frame into a transactional practicum, are scarce. They are also expensive to recruit, because the strongest candidates have BigLaw or in-house options. Without a pipeline that funds research time, mentorship, and a visible intellectual community, programs like LEP cannot retain the faculty who give the program its substance.

## The intervention

The Leo Fellowship funds two-year faculty fellowships at CUA Law for scholars working at the intersection of private-law doctrine and transactional practice. Each fellow receives one course release per year, a research stipend, conference travel, and a research assistant. Fellows participate in the LEP intellectual community: monthly cohort coffee with students, the year-theme event series, and the *societas* working-paper group. The fellowship also funds a visiting-scholar slot each year (semester-long) for a senior scholar in the tradition (Salamanca-school history, Catholic social thought, Jewish commercial law, or modern law-and-religion). Junior LEP faculty are paired with the visiting scholar for substantive mentorship.

## Deliverables

1. Two named Leo Fellows on the faculty page by August 2027, each producing one peer-reviewed article and one practitioner-facing piece per fellowship year.
2. One visiting Senior Leo Scholar per academic year, with a public lecture and a faculty workshop.
3. A *societas* working-paper series (digital), with three to five papers per year.
4. A junior-faculty mentorship structure (monthly meetings) documented in writing.
5. A retention metric: each Leo Fellow's tenure-track or contract path at CUA Law tracked through Year 5.

## Timeline

**Year 1 (AY 2026-27):** Recruit the first Leo Fellow. Launch the working-paper series with three papers. First visiting scholar arrives spring 2027.

**Year 2 (AY 2027-28):** Recruit the second Leo Fellow. Second visiting scholar. First peer-reviewed articles published.

**Year 3 (AY 2028-29):** Retention review of first Leo Fellow. Third cohort of working papers. First junior-faculty mentee completes tenure-track milestone.

## Budget

Three-year total: **$750,000 to $1.2M**.

| Line item | Three-year range |
|---|---|
| Faculty time (two fellowships, course release plus stipend) | $360,000 to $540,000 |
| Visiting Senior Leo Scholar (semester, three years) | $180,000 to $270,000 |
| Research assistants (two per year, three years) | $90,000 to $135,000 |
| Programming (working-paper series, workshops) | $60,000 to $90,000 |
| Dissemination (publication, web, convenings) | $30,000 to $45,000 |
| Indirect costs (15 percent) | $108,000 to $162,000 |

## Metrics

Articles published (peer-reviewed and practitioner). Working papers posted. Visiting scholars hosted. Junior faculty mentored to tenure milestone. Retention of fellows at CUA Law or placement at peer institutions if they leave. Annual report to the funder.

## The problem

Law schools outside the top twenty face a recruiting and retention gap in private-law faculty who can teach contracts, business associations, securities, and commercial law as the operating system of entrepreneurial capital formation. The strongest candidates have transactional-practice options that pay multiples of academic salary; the academic candidates who remain are often trained to write for doctrinal-publication metrics that do not reward integration with practice. The result is a private-law professoriate at programs like LEP that struggles to staff a transactional curriculum at the depth entrepreneurial students need.

## The intervention

The Entrepreneurial Private Law Fellowship funds two-year faculty fellowships at CUA Law for scholars working at the intersection of private-law doctrine and transactional practice. Each fellow receives one course release per year, a research stipend, conference travel, and a research assistant. Fellows participate in LEP's intellectual community: monthly cohort coffee with students, the year-theme event series, and a working-paper group on the law of partnership and capital deployment. The fellowship also funds a visiting-scholar slot each year (semester-long) for a senior scholar in commercial-law history, the institutional economics of partnership, or the comparative law of entrepreneurial finance. Junior fellows are paired with the visiting scholar.

The intellectual commitment of the fellowship is concrete. Partnership at shared risk is the form of capital deployment in which entrepreneur and investor share both upside and downside, and it is the institutional form that has generated the most productive innovation across centuries. The fellowship trains scholars and teachers to treat that institutional form as substantive doctrine.

## Deliverables

1. Two named fellows on the faculty page by August 2027, each producing one peer-reviewed article and one practitioner-facing piece per fellowship year.
2. One visiting senior scholar per academic year, with a public lecture and a faculty workshop.
3. A working-paper series on partnership, capital deployment, and entrepreneurial law, with three to five papers per year.
4. A documented junior-faculty mentorship structure.
5. A five-year retention metric tracking each fellow's path.

## Timeline, budget, and metrics

Identical to the Catholic version above. Three-year total: **$750,000 to $1.2M**, same line items.
