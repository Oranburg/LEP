# The LEP Badge

<p class="section-desc">Board brief 1 of 5 · Priorities for AY 2026-27</p>

**A verifiable micro-credential in entrepreneurship law.** Students earn a badge by demonstrating specific transactional skills: forming an entity, drafting a founder agreement, structuring a financing, running a regulatory analysis. The badge is portable and verifiable, so an employer can confirm the competence directly rather than infer it from a transcript.

## Why now

A transcript certifies that a student attended classes. It does not certify that the student can produce a working formation package or draft an enforceable founder agreement. Employers know this and run their own diligence at the interview stage, which makes the diploma a weak signal of transactional-practice readiness. A badge closes that gap and gives the Columbus School of Law a distinctive, market-readable credential ahead of peer schools.

## The plan

- Unveil the badge design and the first badge in entrepreneurship law this year.
- Issue badges through an outside platform and supplier, so the registrar's office carries no new administrative load.
- Pilot the first badge with a current cohort, then widen the suite to the core transactional tasks.

<div class="start-here-cta">
<strong>The board ask.</strong> Approve the badge concept and a modest annual line of about $1,500 for the verification platform. The annual cost is what keeps the work with an external supplier and off the registrar's desk. We raise it now so the board can decide whether to underwrite it.
</div>
