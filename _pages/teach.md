# Teach with the Program

For academic colleagues: fellow scholars, peer business-law and legal-ethics faculty, and visiting researchers who want to teach, contribute, or collaborate.

## The intellectual frame, briefly

The program teaches business law through the *societas* tradition: the conceptual frame that runs from Roman commercial law and Aquinas through the Salamanca school to the modern Catholic social encyclicals, with the parallel Jewish tradition's *shutafut* doctrine reaching the same conclusion through independent reasoning. See the [Lineage]({{ '/lineage/' | relative_url }}), [Societas]({{ '/societas/' | relative_url }}), and [Magnifica Humanitas]({{ '/magnifica-humanitas/' | relative_url }}) pages for the doctrinal treatment.

The frame is substantive doctrine, not branding. The program's claim is that the partnership-versus-loan distinction in the Aquinas tradition is the conceptual structure of partnership and capital-deployment law as it actually operates today; it gives a lawyer trained in the tradition a way to read the six-provision question of a Series Seed term sheet that a lawyer not so trained cannot.

## What the program is interested in from peer scholars

**Working-paper engagement.** The current working papers are *Architects of the Void* (the entrepreneurial lawyer as sub-creator who constitutes the legal forms within which commercial life takes shape; reads transactional drafting through Maimonides on *ẓedakah*, Aristotle on *phronesis*, Tolkien on sub-creation, and Cover on jurisgenesis; in submission) and *In the Shadow of the Law* (pedagogy essay on the operational-to-doctrinal passage in mid-career and hybrid J.D. populations, naming the passage the Valley of Despair and reading it through Maimonides on habituated opinion, Aquinas on the *ordo disciplinae*, and the cognitive-science literature on desirable difficulties; in submission). Direct citation correspondence and commentary are welcome. Drafts are not circulated; the paper-by-paper status notes on the [Working Papers page]({{ '/working-papers/' | relative_url }}) describe the argument in enough detail to support pre-publication engagement.

**Symposium and panel participation.** The 2026–27 year theme is *Entrepreneurship: Curing Poverty*. The four-event slate (Magatte Wade keynote; SBA capital-policy panel; antitrust panel on JetBlue/Spirit; practitioner panel on transactional drafting) and the *Law and New Things* scholarly symposium (co-convened with Robert T. Miller, publishing in the *Catholic University Law Review*) run through AY 2026-27. Speakers are still being added; correspondence on participation is welcome.

**Visiting-scholar arrangements.** The Leo Fellowship in Entrepreneurial Lawyering (in development) supports visiting-scholar slots. Reach out to discuss fit, timing, and topic. The program is particularly interested in scholars working in private law (contracts, business associations, securities, commercial law) who treat partnership and capital deployment as substantive doctrine.

**Co-authoring.** The program's working-paper pipeline has room for co-authored projects, particularly in three places: the *Laborem Exercens / exercitor navis* article currently in folder embargo pending the Salamanca-school recovery search; the engagement with *Magnifica Humanitas*; and a forthcoming piece on Maimonides and *shutafut* as the Jewish parallel to *societas*.

**Cross-program co-hosting.** The program co-hosts events with the Cluny Institute (also at CUA, run by Luke Burgis) where their themes meet. The Institute's flagship ZOË Conference (July 26 to 28, 2026, Napa) is the immediate co-program-able event.

## What the program offers visitors and contributors

- A scholarly community organized around the *societas* / *shutafut* tradition as substance, not posture
- A clinic with real client matters that supports applied scholarship
- An institutional home at CUA Columbus School of Law in Washington, with the regulatory, congressional, and practitioner networks the location supplies
- Connection to the Cluny Institute's broader Catholic-intellectual-and-technological network
- Working-paper review and pre-submission feedback among the program's faculty
