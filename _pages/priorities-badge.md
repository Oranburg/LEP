# Priority: The LEP Badge

The program will issue a verifiable credential, *the LEP Badge*, certifying that a student has completed the entrepreneurship track through the clinic. Inaugural issuance by the end of AY 2026-27. Small at launch by design. Built to scale.

## What the badge certifies

Completion of the LEP entrepreneurship track is defined by four components:

1. **Doctrinal coursework.** Business Associations, a transactional elective (Securities Regulation or comparable), and one additional course in the LEP track.
2. **Supervised drafting work.** At least three substantive transactional deliverables produced under faculty supervision.
3. **Client deliverables.** Service to at least two clinic clients to documented completion, with clinical faculty sign-off.
4. **Reflection.** A one-page reflection on the practical-doctrinal passage the LEP track is designed to produce.

A student who satisfies all four components receives the badge. Partial credit does not stack.

## The technical standard

The badge is issued under the W3C Verifiable Credentials standard, conforming to Open Badges 3.0. The credential is signed by CUA Columbus Law through the program, anchored to a public verification path that any third party can check without contacting the issuer, portable across institutions and platforms, and displayable on LinkedIn through LinkedIn's existing verifiable-credentials integration. The standard is the same one under which state bars, professional certification organizations, and major university programs increasingly issue credentials.

## Cost and vendor

The recurring cost is in the range of $1,500 per year if vendor-paid. The program is finalizing the vendor choice between Brightspace's D2L Awards (already in the CUA learning management system at no incremental cost) and Credly (which several peer Catholic universities use). The decision is sequenced through the Provost's office, which holds the first-order question of whether CUA already has a credentialing vendor for the institution as a whole.

The premise is no burden on the registrar's office. The vendor cost buys that.

## Scaling

The inaugural badge is one. The program commits to scaling the badge portfolio along two dimensions: additional badges for additional programs (a transactional simulation course, mentorship cohort completion, symposium participation, working-paper contribution), and tiered credentials within an existing badge family (a *with distinction* tier introduced in Year 3, defined by the clinical faculty against deliverable quality and client outcomes).

## What the program asks of the Board

The Board is not asked to approve the badge. The badge is a program-level decision communicated to the Board. The Board is asked to advise on the credential's positioning with the wider Washington legal community, on the recognition pattern the program should expect from local employers, and on the credential's relationship to the bar's own credential structure.
