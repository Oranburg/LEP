# Priorities for AY 2026-27

The program's directors have agreed on five priorities for the coming year. Each is summarized below in a single page, suitable for circulation in advance of the Fall 2026 Board of Visitors meeting and to the Law School Alumni Council. The detail is at the linked one-pagers; the asks of the Board are stated where they apply.

The priorities sit inside the year theme, [*Entrepreneurship: Curing Poverty*]({{ '/theme/' | relative_url }}), and inside the program's longer argument at [The Program's Argument]({{ '/argument/' | relative_url }}).

## The five

1. **[The LEP Badge.]({{ '/priorities/badge/' | relative_url }})** A verifiable credential certifying student completion of the entrepreneurship program through the clinic. Inaugural issuance by end of AY 2026-27.
2. **[Private-law faculty.]({{ '/priorities/faculty/' | relative_url }})** One more clinical course, one more experiential course, and a curricular-spectrum map across podium, experiential, clinical, and seminar offerings.
3. **[Business law mentorship.]({{ '/priorities/mentorship/' | relative_url }})** A structured pairing of LEP students with practicing transactional lawyers in the Washington market. The program's ask of the Board.
4. **[Year theme.]({{ '/priorities/theme/' | relative_url }})** *Entrepreneurship: Curing Poverty.* Magatte Wade keynote, federal capital-formation panel, antitrust worked example, transactional-drafting panel, and the *Law and New Things* symposium.
5. **[Toward a Catholic Business Law Center?]({{ '/priorities/center/' | relative_url }})** A five-year horizon. The fellowship model already running at CUA Columbus Law, with fellowships for students and future faculty alike, and topics beyond entrepreneurship that the longer arc takes up.

## How the priorities relate

The Badge is the credential the program issues at the end of the track. The Faculty priority is what makes the track substantively richer. The Mentorship priority is what converts the doctrinal and clinical preparation into careers. The Year Theme is the intellectual frame the year's events express. The Center question is the five-year shape of all four.

Each priority is communicated to the Board as a discrete piece. The Board is asked to react to each, to advise on each, and to take on one specific role: to mentor a student under the third priority and to identify two additional candidates from the Board members' own networks.
