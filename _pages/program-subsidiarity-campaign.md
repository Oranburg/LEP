# Subsidiarity Campaign

An infrastructure campaign that funds the doctrinal-experiential-clinical-placement pipeline: the substantive curriculum, the Business Boot Camp, the Transactional Practicum, the clinic deepening, and the placement and mentorship layer. The size of the institutional-capacity ask.

## The problem

The doctrine-to-practice pipeline at most law schools is broken in a specific way. Substantive courses (Contracts, Business Associations, Securities, Tax) are taught in one part of the curriculum. Experiential learning (simulations, drafting exercises) lives in another. Clinical practice (real-client representation) lives in a third. Placement and professional formation live with the career office. Students walk through these stages as if they were separate buildings. The result is a graduate who has studied doctrine, completed a simulation, served clients, and accepted a job offer without any single faculty member or program having carried the student through the full arc. *Quadragesimo Anno* (Pius XI, 1931) names the principle that resolves this: subsidiarity, the doctrine that a higher institution should support, not replace, the work of the lower. The campaign rebuilds the pipeline so that the doctrine, the simulation, the clinic, and the placement form one continuous formation.

## The intervention

Infrastructure-building across four connected components.

1. A 1L Business Boot Camp (one credit, pass-fail, modeled on Brooklyn) as the on-ramp.
2. A 2L transactional practicum (two credits, Case Western LLEAP 3 model) running one deal from term sheet to closing.
3. A deepened entrepreneurial clinic with subject-matter focus (Cardozo model).
4. A placement and professional-formation layer (mentorship pairings, alumni network, DC regulatory and firm externship pipeline) that connects the cohort to the bar after graduation.

## Deliverables

1. Business Boot Camp on the 1L registration menu, fall 2026, capped at twenty-five, taught annually.
2. Transactional practicum approved and on the registration menu by fall 2027 for spring 2028 launch, capped at twenty, taught annually.
3. Entrepreneurial Clinic with documented subject-matter focus, stable client pipeline (ten to fifteen clients per year), and a redacted-document portfolio students take into interviews.
4. Practitioner-mentor pool covering six categories (BigLaw transactional, in-house GC at growth venture, fintech regulatory, community-business transactional, federal honors path, entrepreneur-counsel) with named pairings.
5. A documented placement track from cohort to first-job, recorded for the first three cohorts.

## Timeline

**Year 1 (AY 2026-27):** Boot Camp launches. Clinic subject-matter focus documented. Mentor pool to six named practitioners. First cohort coffee in September 2026.

**Year 2 (AY 2027-28):** Transactional practicum launches. Clinic stabilizes at twelve clients. Mentor pool to twelve named practitioners. Second cohort enters.

**Year 3 (AY 2028-29):** All four components operating. First cohort placement data collected. Clinic at fifteen to twenty clients. Mentor pool to eighteen named practitioners.

## Budget

Three-year total: **$900,000 to $1.5M**.

| Line item | Three-year range |
|---|---|
| Faculty time (Boot Camp, practicum, clinic capacity) | $300,000 to $450,000 |
| Adjuncts and practitioner instructors | $90,000 to $135,000 |
| Clinic infrastructure (case management, intake, recruitment) | $180,000 to $270,000 |
| Programming (cohort coffees, mentor events, placement outreach) | $90,000 to $135,000 |
| Dissemination (program web, placement reporting, alumni network) | $60,000 to $90,000 |
| Indirect costs (15 percent) | $108,000 to $180,000 |

## Metrics

Boot Camp enrollment and 1L-to-LEP cohort conversion. Practicum enrollment and student portfolios. Clinic client count and deliverable archive. Mentor pool size and meeting compliance (target: two per semester). Placement data (employer category, geography, practice area) for each cohort at three months, nine months, and three years post-graduation.

## When to bring this proposal forward

The Subsidiarity Campaign is the larger institutional ask. It is the natural escalation if a funder wants institutional-capacity scale. For DDSF, the recommended posture is to lead with [Entrepreneurship v.
