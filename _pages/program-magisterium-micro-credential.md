# Magisterium Micro-Credential Curriculum

A skill-based micro-credential program in which students earn verifiable badges for demonstrated ability to do specific transactional tasks. Portable to employers; verifiable through the program. The innovation hook in the funding portfolio.

## The problem

Law school transcripts certify that a student attended classes. They do not certify that the student can produce a working formation package, draft an enforceable founder agreement, analyze a Reg D Rule 506(b) offering, sign off on a 409A valuation review, or classify an employment relationship correctly. Employers know this and run their own diligence at the interview stage, which makes the diploma a weak signal for transactional-practice readiness. The Catholic teaching tradition has a name for the body of certified, verifiable competence the Church carries through its institutions: the magisterium. The program builds an analogous portable credential at the level of specific transactional tasks.

## The intervention

A skill-based micro-credential program. Students earn verifiable badges for demonstrated ability on specific transactional tasks. The initial badge set covers: formation package, founder agreement with vesting, SAFE or convertible-note structuring, Reg D Rule 506(b) analysis, 409A valuation review, board consent drafting, employment classification, fintech or digital-asset regulatory analysis, and customer or vendor MSA. Each badge is earned by completing a graded exercise reviewed by a faculty member and a practitioner. Badges are issued on a portable, verifiable platform (Open Badges or equivalent) so students carry the credential into the job market.

## Deliverables

1. A badge framework document (nine to twelve badges) with grading rubrics, published by spring 2027.
2. The first three badges live and earnable by 2L cohort, spring 2027.
3. The full badge suite live by spring 2028.
4. A verification platform (Open Badges or equivalent) with public verification URLs for each issued badge.
5. An employer-side adoption track: ten to twenty employers (firms, in-house, agencies) who recognize the badges in hiring conversations by spring 2029.

## Timeline

**Year 1 (AY 2026-27):** Badge framework drafted. First three badges piloted with one cohort. Platform selected and configured.

**Year 2 (AY 2027-28):** Full badge suite live. First issued badges to graduating students. First employer-side conversations.

**Year 3 (AY 2028-29):** Full suite operating. First cohort placement data tied to badge holdings. Employer-side adoption tracked.

## Budget

Three-year total: **$400,000 to $650,000**.

| Line item | Three-year range |
|---|---|
| Faculty time (badge design, rubric authoring, review) | $120,000 to $180,000 |
| Practitioner reviewers (honoraria) | $60,000 to $90,000 |
| Platform (Open Badges, configuration, hosting) | $30,000 to $45,000 |
| Programming (badge-earning workshops, employer outreach) | $90,000 to $135,000 |
| Dissemination (framework publication, web, employer documentation) | $48,000 to $72,000 |
| Indirect costs (15 percent) | $52,200 to $85,000 |

## Metrics

Badges designed and live. Badges earned per cohort. Employer recognition rate. Placement correlation: graduates with badges versus without. Annual report.

## How this fits the funding portfolio

The Magisterium Micro-Credential is the smallest of the four programs and the most market-readable. Foundations value the verifiability and the measurable outcome (placement correlation). It is a strong standalone if a funder wants to test a relationship at a smaller initial scale, and a strong companion to the larger campaigns.
