# Business Law Careers Mentorship Program

<p class="section-desc">Board brief 3 of 5 · Priorities for AY 2026-27</p>

**A mentorship and placement network in private-law practice. The program's clearest request of the board.** The program connects students with practitioners in business and transactional law, in the DC area and beyond: mentors, externship hosts, and employers. The work builds the second of the two pipelines on which the program depends.

## Why now

Success rests on two pipelines: faculty to teach, and jobs for students to take. The second is the harder one. Catholic University graduates are under-represented among private-law hiring partners in the DC area. Closing that gap calls for networks that reach where the program's own do not yet, and the board's networks reach exactly there.

## The plan

- Build a roster of practitioner mentors and hiring partners in business and transactional law.
- Pair cohort students with mentors, and route students toward externships and first positions.
- Grow the alumni hiring network deliberately, year over year, in the DC market first.

<div class="start-here-cta">
<strong>The board ask.</strong> This is a request, not a report. The board sits in the firms, in-house teams, and alumni circles the program most needs to reach. We ask members to open doors: introductions to private-law employers in the DC area, and to alumni practicing in transactional fields. The network may be reachable only with the board's help.
</div>
