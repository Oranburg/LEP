# Support the Program

The program is built to outperform its institutional ranking through cohort depth, clinical practice, and mentorship infrastructure rather than through endowment scale. That posture is also why it is supported by named, structured gifts tied to specific deliverables, rather than by general operations.

The four programs below carry the full picture of what the program is being built to do. Each is available in two parallel framings (Catholic and secular), with the same substance, deliverables, timeline, budget, and metrics. The framing changes the vocabulary; the program does not.

## How the framings work

Each program page above carries the same content in two parallel framings. The Catholic framing names the doctrinal sources (Aquinas, Maimonides, *Quadragesimo Anno*, *Magnifica Humanitas*) and is the form to share with Catholic foundations, Jewish-philanthropic networks, and Templeton-adjacent funders. The secular framing carries the same program substance in the vocabulary of free enterprise, principled entrepreneurship, institutional decentralization, and classical-liberal pluralism; it is the form to share with the Diana Davis Spencer Foundation and similar funders.

The toggle on each page lets a reader switch between the two without leaving the page. The choice persists across the site, so a funder reading multiple program pages can stay in one framing.

## Other gift opportunities

### The LEP Fellowship (student)

A dedicated student fellowship that carries stipend support, conference travel, and research funding for a small number of cohort students per class. The Fellowship is in development. Gift opportunities range from named annual fellowships to endowed perpetual fellowships.

### Named lectureships and event sponsorship

The inaugural year-theme (and successive year-themes) each include a named keynote, a panel series, and a closing symposium. Single-event sponsorship is available; named-lectureship sponsorship across successive years is the deeper engagement.

### Clinic naming and clinical fellow

The Law and Entrepreneurship Clinic is being deepened in AY 2026-27. Naming opportunities exist for the clinic itself, for a postgraduate clinical fellow position, and for a practitioner-in-residence visiting attorney post.
