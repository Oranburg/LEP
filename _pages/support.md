# Support the Program

A small set of named gift opportunities, each tied to a deliverable the program has designed and is in development to deliver. The program is built to outperform its institutional ranking through cohort depth, clinical practice, and mentorship infrastructure rather than through endowment scale; that is also why it is supported by named, structured gifts rather than by general operations.

## Gift opportunities, by program

### Leo Fellowship in Entrepreneurial Lawyering

A faculty-development initiative that recruits, supports, and retains private-law faculty (contracts, business associations, securities, commercial law) who treat the law of partnership and capital deployment as substantive doctrine. The Fellowship funds research time, conference travel, mentorship of junior faculty, and visiting-scholar slots. Multi-year, named in honor of Pope Leo XIV's *Magnifica Humanitas*. *Available in a secular framing for funders who prefer it.*

### Entrepreneurship v. Poverty Year-Theme

The inaugural year-theme runs through four convening events (Magatte Wade keynote on entrepreneurship as the answer to poverty; SBA capital-policy panel; antitrust panel on JetBlue/Spirit; practitioner panel on transactional drafting) with associated working papers and clinic projects. Single-year sponsorship or multi-year sponsorship across successive year-themes. *Available in a secular framing.*

### Subsidiarity Campaign

An infrastructure campaign that funds the doctrinal-experiential-clinical pipeline: the substantive curriculum, the Business Boot Camp, the Transactional Practicum, the clinic deepening, and placement infrastructure. Modeled on the moves made by Cardozo, Case Western, and Brooklyn to punch above their institutional rank. *Available in a secular framing as the "Distributed Practice Campaign."*

### Magisterium Micro-Credential Curriculum

A skill-based micro-credential program in which students earn verifiable badges for demonstrated ability to do specific transactional tasks (formation packages, founder agreements, SAFE structuring, Reg D analysis, board consents, regulatory analysis, employment classification). Portable to employers; verifiable through the program. *Available in a secular framing as the "Practitioner Credential."*

## The LEP Fellowship (Student)

A dedicated student fellowship that carries stipend support, conference travel, and research funding for a small number of cohort students per class. The Fellowship is in development. Gift opportunities range from named annual fellowships to endowed perpetual fellowships.

## Named lectureship and event sponsorship

The inaugural year-theme and successive year-themes each include a named keynote, a panel series, and a closing symposium. Single-event sponsorship is available; named-lectureship sponsorship across successive years is the deeper engagement.

## Clinic and externship pipeline

The Law and Entrepreneurship Clinic is being deepened in AY 2026-27 with explicit subject-matter focus on entrepreneurial and community business representation. Naming opportunities exist for the clinic itself, for the clinical fellow (a postgraduate clinical staff position), and for the practitioner-in-residence visiting attorney post.

## How to engage

For gift conversations: Peggy Kacergis, Assistant Dean of Advancement at CUA Columbus School of Law, kacergis@cua.edu. For program-specific giving discussions copy the program directors at hoffarth@cua.edu.

Detailed program one-pagers (with budgets, timelines, deliverables, and metrics) are available on request. Each one-pager exists in two parallel framings (a Catholic intellectual framing and a secular institutional framing) to fit the funder's stated giving register.
