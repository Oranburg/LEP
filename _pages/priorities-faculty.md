# Priority: Private-Law Faculty

The program needs at least one more clinical course and one more experiential course to support the next cohort. Beyond the next hire, the program needs a curricular-spectrum map across podium, experiential, clinical, and seminar courses, and a faculty plan that supplies each layer.

## The near-term hire

A Visiting Assistant Clinical Professor appointment, beginning Fall 2026, supports the expanded clinical intake. The clinic's current trajectory carries it past the capacity of a single supervising professor; the second clinical line is the operational move that lets the inaugural badge cohort be served at the standard the badge promises.

The longer-term move converts a successful visiting line into a tenure-track or longer-term clinical line, depending on the Year 1 evaluation.

## The curricular spectrum

The program teaches across four layers, each requiring faculty whose career trajectory fits the layer.

| Layer | What it teaches | Who teaches it |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Podium | Doctrinal courses (Business Associations, Securities Regulation, Federal Income Tax) | Tenured and tenure-track faculty |
| Experiential | Simulation and drafting courses (Transactional Drafting, Venture Finance, Deal Structuring) | Practitioners with current transactional practice; adjunct or visiting status |
| Clinical | Live-client representation through the entrepreneurship clinic | Clinical faculty on tenure track or longer-term contracts |
| Seminar | Topical seminars (Catholic Social Thought and the Corporation, Law and New Things) | Tenured faculty with substantive research agendas in the area |

The current faculty serves the podium and clinical layers well. The experiential and seminar layers are where the program builds out.

## Student demand

The next cohort's distribution of interest, measured by 1L course enrollment and by the program's intake conversations, supports two to three additional experiential offerings (Venture Finance, Deal Structuring, Transactional Drafting) and one additional seminar (the Law and New Things subject matter as a standing seminar). Student demand is the calibration variable.

## The leadership-trajectory question

The growth path requires, at some point, a clinical director who manages the clinical faculty as a team. The directors will surface this question at the appropriate moment, internal to the law school's faculty processes. It does not require Board action.

## What the program asks of the Board

The Board is asked to:

1. React to the curricular-spectrum map and identify gaps the directors have not surfaced.
2. Help identify experiential-layer faculty candidates from the Washington transactional bar, particularly practitioners whose career trajectory makes them strong adjunct or visiting candidates.
3. Advise on the sequencing of the next two clinical hires against the broader hiring situation at Columbus Law.
