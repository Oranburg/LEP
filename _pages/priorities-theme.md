# Priority: Year Theme

The 2026-27 LEP year theme is *Entrepreneurship: Curing Poverty*. The colon does real work: this is a thesis posture, not a contested-case posture. Entrepreneurship is the activity that cures poverty. The year takes that as the working position and asks what the lawyer does to advance it.

The full intellectual statement of the theme is at [*Entrepreneurship: Curing Poverty*]({{ '/theme/' | relative_url }}). The summary below covers what the year *does* with the theme.

## The thesis

The two-hundred-year record of capital-for-labor substitution shows the cure is real. The World Bank record on extreme poverty (37.8 percent of the world population in 1990, 8.4 percent in 2019) measures the same claim at global scale. Magatte Wade's *The Heart of a Cheetah* is the contemporary statement. The Aquinas-Salamanca tradition reaches the same conclusion from the moral-theology side. Maimonides reaches it through the eight rungs of *tzedakah*, with partnership at the top.

The lawyer's question is which structures, drafted by which lawyers under which intellectual frames, actually cure poverty in measurable ways, and which produce the appearance of partnership while extracting the upside.

## The event slate

The theme runs through a slate of four public convening events plus the program's scholarly symposium.

1. **Magatte Wade keynote** on entrepreneurship as the cure to poverty. Anchor event; opens the year.
2. **Federal small-business and capital-formation panel** on which design features actually produce entrepreneurial agency among the poor.
3. **Charlie Beller (DOJ Antitrust) on the JetBlue / Spirit case** as a worked example of when concentration produces the conditions for partnership and when it forecloses them.
4. **Practitioner panel on transactional drafting** on what the partnership form looks like in the documents lawyers actually draft.

## The symposium

The *Law and New Things* symposium at CUA Columbus Law sits inside the year theme as the scholarly convening. Co-convened with Robert T. Miller (Scalia Law). Publishing in the *Catholic University Law Review* 2026-27 symposium edition. The symposium asks the same partnership-or-extraction question of the new technologies (data, algorithms, foundation models, AI labor markets) that the year theme asks of the historical record.

## The frame in one sentence

The lawyer trained in the *societas* tradition can read a federal small-business policy, a venture-capital term sheet, an antitrust decision, or an AI-platform services agreement and ask the same question of each: does this preserve partnership at shared risk, or does it collapse partnership into creditor extraction in which the upside belongs to one party and the downside to another?

## What the program asks of the Board

The Board is asked to:

1. React to the year's topical idea and to the four-event slate.
2. Propose subsequent years' themes. The program intends a multi-year sequence of topical years; the Board's input shapes which topics come next.
3. Attend the events. The Board members' presence at the keynote and the symposium is the public marker that the program operates with institutional weight.
