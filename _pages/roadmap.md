# Roadmap

The Program has a fixed argument and a dated build. The argument does not change. Lawyers make private law in drafting, under the substantive norm of partnership at shared risk, by formation methods recovered from three surviving traditions, against the live constraint of the federal regulatory state, at the one American law school where the Catholic doctrinal inheritance and the Washington setting converge. [The Program's Argument]({{ '/argument/' | relative_url }}) states it in full. The build is the three-year sequence by which that argument becomes a curriculum, a clinic, a faculty, and a credential.

Four streams run at once. Each funds a different part of the same formation, and each carries its own deliverables, budget, and metrics on its program page.

| Stream | What it builds | Spine element it serves | Detail |
|---|---|---|---|
| Curriculum and cohort | Business Boot Camp, Transactional Practicum, the *societas* reading lens | I and II: lawyers make private law, under the partnership norm | [Subsidiarity Campaign]({{ '/support/subsidiarity-campaign/' | relative_url }}) |
| Clinic | Deepened entrepreneurial clinic for low-and-moderate-income founders | II and IV: the norm applied, against the regulatory state | [Entrepreneurship: Curing Poverty]({{ '/support/entrepreneurship-v-poverty/' | relative_url }}) |
| Faculty | Two-year Leo Fellowships, visiting senior scholar, working-paper series | III: formation of the people who teach the formation | [Leo Fellowship]({{ '/support/leo-fellowship/' | relative_url }}) |
| Credential | Portable, verifiable transactional-skill badges | I: the drafting act, certified at task level | [Magisterium Micro-Credential]({{ '/support/magisterium-micro-credential/' | relative_url }}) |

The clinic operates now under founding Director Alexander Hoffarth. Seth Oranburg joins as co-director on August 20, 2026. The dated build below begins with the first full year both co-directors are in place.

## The durable structure and the rotating theme

The spine does not move from year to year. The year theme does. Each academic year takes one contested question and runs the spine through it. The inaugural theme for AY 2026-27 is [*Entrepreneurship: Curing Poverty*]({{ '/theme/' | relative_url }}), which asks which structures, drafted by which lawyers, actually cure poverty by partnership, and which produce the appearance of partnership while extracting the upside. Later years rotate the question. The build below carries the structure that holds across every theme.

## Short term: AY 2026-27, the on-ramp year

The year that puts the first components on the registration menu and the first cohort in the room.

- **Business Boot Camp** on the 1L registration menu, fall 2026. One credit, pass-fail, capped at twenty-five. The on-ramp into the cohort.
- **First cohort coffee**, September 2026.
- **Year-theme event slate**: the Magatte Wade keynote on entrepreneurship as the cure to poverty, a federal capital-formation policy panel, a DOJ Antitrust panel on the JetBlue / Spirit decision, and a practitioner panel on transactional drafting. The [Events page]({{ '/events/' | relative_url }}) carries the calendar as dates confirm.
- **Law and New Things symposium**, November or December 2026, co-convened with Robert T. Miller, publishing in the *Catholic University Law Review*. The scholarly convening inside the year theme. See [Law and New Things]({{ '/essays/law-and-new-things/' | relative_url }}).
- **Clinic** deepened to roughly ten active entrepreneurial ventures, subject-matter focus documented.
- **Mentor pool** to six named practitioners across the six categories.
- **Faculty**: first Leo Fellow recruited; the *societas* working-paper series launches with three papers; the first visiting senior scholar arrives spring 2027.
- **Credential**: badge framework drafted, first three badges piloted with one cohort, verification platform selected.
- **Writing**: the year-theme essay published; the *societas* curriculum-overlay document drafted.

## Medium term: AY 2027-28, the deal year

The year the pipeline becomes continuous, from the doctrine through a closed deal.

- **Transactional Practicum** launches, spring 2028, on the registration menu by fall 2027. Two credits, capped at twenty, one deal run from term sheet to closing.
- **Clinic** to twelve, then fifteen active clients. First one-year client outcome data collected.
- **Curriculum overlay** in three courses.
- **Mentor pool** to twelve named practitioners. Second cohort enters.
- **Faculty**: second Leo Fellow recruited; second visiting scholar; first peer-reviewed articles from the first fellow published.
- **Credential**: full badge suite live; first badges issued to graduating students; first employer-side conversations.

## Long term: AY 2028-29, the evidence year

The year the build is complete and the outcomes come in.

- **All four pipeline components operating**: doctrine, Boot Camp, practicum, clinic, placement.
- **Clinic** stabilized at fifteen to twenty clients. First three-year client outcome data.
- **Curriculum overlay** in five courses.
- **Placement**: first cohort placement data collected at three months, nine months, and three years.
- **Mentor pool** to eighteen named practitioners.
- **Faculty**: retention review of the first Leo Fellow; third cohort of working papers; first junior-faculty tenure-track milestone.
- **Credential**: full suite operating; placement correlated to badge holdings; ten to twenty employers recognizing the badges by spring 2029.

## What three years is meant to produce

A graduate who entered through the Boot Camp, drafted a deal in the practicum, served entrepreneurial clients in the clinic under a mentor, carried a portable record of demonstrated transactional skill, and was taught the whole arc by faculty formed to teach private law as the law of partnership at shared risk. The measure on the clinic side is poverty reduced, not inequality adjusted: job creation, revenue, and ventures still operating at one year and three years. The [methodology]({{ '/methodology/' | relative_url }}) holds that distinction firm.
