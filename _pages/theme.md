# Entrepreneurship: Curing Poverty

The 2026–27 LEP year theme, agreed by the program's directors, is *Entrepreneurship: Curing Poverty*. The title states the thesis the year tests. Entrepreneurship is the activity that cures poverty. The two-hundred-year record of capital-for-labor substitution shows it. The question the year takes up is the lawyer's question: which structures, drafted by which lawyers under which intellectual frames, actually cure poverty in measurable ways, and which produce the appearance of partnership while extracting the upside.

The thesis is not new. Magatte Wade's *The Heart of a Cheetah* is the contemporary statement. The Aquinas–Salamanca tradition reaches the same conclusion from the moral-theology side. Maimonides reaches it through the eight rungs of *tzedakah*, with partnership at the top. The World Bank record on extreme poverty (37.8 percent of the world population in 1990, 8.4 percent in 2019) measures the same claim at the global scale.

The year takes the thesis as the working position and asks what the lawyer does to advance it. The substantive norm is partnership at shared risk, which is the form of capital deployment under which the entrepreneur and the worker share the upside and the downside together. The diagnostic counterpart is the structure that hides under partnership language while operating as fixed-return extraction; Leo XIV names the AI-age version of that structure in *Magnifica Humanitas* §§ 173 to 178 (hidden labor chains, data colonialism). The cure the year endorses is not a slogan against those structures. It is the affirmative practice of drafting the partnership form so the recipient's hand is strengthened, in the Maimonidean sense, by the document that names the relationship.

The lawyer at the founding moment is a sub-creator who constitutes the institutional body in which entrepreneurial work becomes possible. The same drafting capacity can build a firm that lets the recipient cease to be poor, or a structure that takes the upside and pushes the downside onto workers, users, or communities. The year-theme is therefore the lawyer's question: which structures cure poverty by partnership, and which cure nothing while calling themselves partnerships.

## The event slate

The year theme runs through a slate of convening events.

1. **Magatte Wade keynote** on entrepreneurship as the cure to poverty.
2. **A panel on recent federal small-business and capital-formation policy** asking which design features actually produce entrepreneurial agency among the poor.
3. **Charlie Beller (DOJ Antitrust) on the JetBlue / Spirit case** as a worked example of when concentration produces the conditions for partnership and when it forecloses them.
4. **A practitioner panel on transactional drafting** asking what the partnership form looks like in the documents lawyers actually draft.

The *Law and New Things* symposium at CUA Columbus Law (November or December 2026; co-convened with Robert T. Miller; *Catholic University Law Review* 2026–27 symposium edition) sits inside the year theme as the scholarly convening: it asks the same partnership-vs-extraction question of the new technologies (data, algorithms, foundation models, AI labor markets). ## How the theme connects to the program's argument

The [Program's Argument]({{ '/argument/' | relative_url }}) sets the spine in five elements: lawyers make private law in drafting, under the substantive norm of partnership at shared risk, by formation methods recovered from three surviving traditions, against the live constraint of the federal regulatory state, at the only American law school where the Catholic doctrinal inheritance and the Washington setting converge. The year theme runs that spine through a single contested terrain. The question of whether entrepreneurship cures poverty is the question of whether the lawyer's drafting actually produces partnerships at shared risk, or only the appearance of them.

The lawyer trained in the *societas* tradition can read a federal SBA program design, a venture-capital term sheet, an antitrust decision, or an AI-platform services agreement and ask the same question of each: does this preserve partnership at shared risk, or does it collapse partnership into creditor extraction in which the upside belongs to one party and the downside to another? The year-theme treats that question as the one that organizes the entire year of programming.
