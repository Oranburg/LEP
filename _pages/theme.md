# *Entrepreneurship v. Poverty*

The inaugural year-theme of the program is written as a case caption. The "v." is doing real work. It names a question to be tried on the record, not a slogan to be affirmed.

Two readings of the same evidence stand opposed.

The first reads entrepreneurship as the answer to poverty: the productive partnership that lets the poor cease to be poor. This is the reading championed by Magatte Wade (*The Heart of a Cheetah*) and by the long tradition of work that treats entrepreneurial agency as the most direct route out of dependence. {% include term-tooltip.html term="Societas" definition="A partnership form at shared risk; within this program's frame it names co-ownership rather than fixed-return debt." context="Specialized term doing core doctrinal work in the program's frame." %} in this reading is straightforwardly good: the partnership form is what enables the dignity of self-sufficient work.

The second reads entrepreneurship as a producer of poverty: {% include term-tooltip.html term="the engine of data colonialism" status="violation" definition="This phrase uses metaphorical machinery imagery. Prefer literal wording unless the essay has explicitly committed to that frame." context="Style check: potential metaphor outside the active frame." %}, gig precarity, and the new forms of dependence that Pope Leo XIV names in *Magnifica Humanitas* (see especially §§ 173 to 178 on hidden labor chains and data colonialism). In this reading, the partnership form is too easily collapsed into a structure that extracts value without sharing it; what looks like *societas* is actually *mutuum*.

The year holds both as adversarial parties and asks which fact patterns lie on which side of the line. The program does not foreclose the question. The convening events, the readings, and the practitioners are arranged so that the evidence on both sides can be heard.

The lawyer sits inside that question rather than outside it. The program's working paper *Architects of the Void* (in submission) names the lawyer at the founding moment as a sub-creator who constitutes the institutional body in which entrepreneurial work becomes possible. The same drafting capacity can build a firm that strengthens the recipient's hand in the Maimonidean sense, or a structure that extracts the upside while pushing the downside onto workers, users, or communities. The year-theme is therefore the lawyer's question as much as it is the economist's or the policymaker's: which structures, drafted by which lawyers under which intellectual frames, produce *societas* and which produce *mutuum* dressed up as partnership.

## The event slate

The inaugural year-theme runs through four convening events:

1. **Magatte Wade keynote** on entrepreneurship as the answer to poverty.
2. **A panel on recent federal small-business and capital-formation policy** asking whether it actually achieves the goal of entrepreneurial agency for the poor or whether it underwrites the structures Leo XIV diagnoses.
3. **Charlie Beller (DOJ Antitrust) on the JetBlue / Spirit case** as a worked example of the question of when concentration produces the conditions for partnership and when it forecloses them.
4. **A practitioner panel on transactional drafting** asking what the partnership form of justice looks like in the actual documents lawyers draft.

The [Events page]({{ '/events/' | relative_url }}) carries the calendar as events are confirmed.

## How the theme connects to the program's doctrinal spine

The *societas* tradition (see the [Societas page]({{ '/societas/' | relative_url }})) provides the conceptual vocabulary for reading the evidence on both sides. The lawyer trained in this tradition can read a federal SBA program design, a venture-capital term sheet, or an antitrust decision and ask the same question of each: does this preserve partnership at shared risk, or does it collapse the partnership into a creditor relationship in which the upside belongs to one party and the downside to another?

The year-theme treats that question as the one that organizes the entire year of programming.
