# *Entrepreneurship v. Poverty*

The year-theme is written as a case caption. The "v." is doing real work. It names a question to be tried on the record, not a slogan to be affirmed. Two readings of the same evidence stand opposed. The first reads entrepreneurship as the answer to poverty: the productive partnership that lets the poor cease to be poor. The second reads entrepreneurship as a producer of poverty: the engine of data colonialism, gig precarity, and the new forms of dependence that Pope Leo XIV names in *Magnifica Humanitas*. The year holds both as adversarial parties and asks which fact patterns lie on which side of the line.

The program does not foreclose the question. The convening events, the readings, and the practitioners are arranged so the evidence on both sides can be heard.
