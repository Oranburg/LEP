# Priority: Business Law Mentorship

The program will pair LEP-affiliated 2Ls and 3Ls with practicing transactional lawyers in the Washington market for the academic year. The mentorship initiative is the operational bridge between the year theme and the careers the program's students go into. It is the program's ask of the Board.

## The problem the initiative addresses

CUA Columbus Law graduates well into federal public-law positions: agency honors programs, DOJ honors programs, congressional offices, judicial clerkships. The institutional infrastructure to support those placements is mature. The transactional pipeline into Washington-market law firms, in-house counsel offices, and corporate-finance practice is the underserved track.

The gap is network density, not doctrinal preparation. Students who arrive interested in transactional practice frequently leave for the public-law track because the transactional network at CUA does not yet convert interest into offers at the rate the institution is capable of. The mentorship initiative addresses the network-density gap directly.

## The structure

Each pairing is anchored by three structured commitments per semester.

1. **One substantive meeting per month.** Sixty to ninety minutes, in person where possible. The program supplies the agenda template; the mentor and student adapt it. Topics run through a curriculum that covers career-path mapping, the practitioner's actual work, a specific transactional matter at the level of detail confidentiality permits, the student's job-search planning, and an unstructured topic of the pair's choosing.
2. **One shadowed practice event per semester.** A deal closing, a financing call, a regulatory engagement, an investment-committee presentation, or another live transactional event at which a 2L or 3L is welcome to observe. The mentor selects the event. The program supplies the confidentiality framework.
3. **One introductory email per semester.** The mentor identifies a person in their network whom the student should know and makes the introduction. The target is not necessarily a job source; the target is a person whose work the student would benefit from understanding.

## Pilot year (AY 2026-27)

Inaugural cohort of ten to fifteen 2Ls and 3Ls. Twelve to eighteen mentors. Inaugural mentorship dinner in late Fall 2026, attended by the inaugural pairings and the Board. End-of-year reflection collection in May 2027. First career-outcome data point (2L summer placements) collected in June and July 2027.

## Measurement

Three time horizons. Year-end metrics measure operational discipline (pairings completed, meetings held, shadowed events held, introductions made). Career-outcome metrics measure the initiative's reason for existing (2L summer placements, post-3L placements, one-year and three-year career posture). Program-shape metrics inform the next year's planning.

## The ask

The mentorship initiative is the operational ask of the Board. The directors ask each Board member to:

1. Mentor one LEP student in AY 2026-27.
2. Identify two additional mentor candidates from the Board member's own network and make introductions to the directors.
3. Attend the inaugural mentorship dinner in late Fall 2026.
4. React to the structure and propose adjustments the Board members consider more useful given their vantage on the practice.

The Board's network is the resource. The Board's volunteer involvement is what converts the resource into operational capacity.
