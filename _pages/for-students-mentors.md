# For mentors, employers, and alumni

You want to give back, hire from the program, or otherwise engage. This page is short on purpose.

## Mentor the program needs

The mentor pool is being built across Summer 2026 for the first AY 2026-27 cohort. Target categories:

- BigLaw transactional partners (DC offices preferred)
- In-house general counsel at growth-stage ventures
- Fintech and digital-asset regulatory counsel
- Federal agency honors-program alumni (SEC, CFTC, FDIC, OCC, FTC, DOJ Antitrust, IRS, CFPB)
- Community business and small-firm transactional lawyers
- Entrepreneur-counsel (founders who carry their own legal-counsel responsibility)
- Catholic-affiliated nonprofit and mission-organization counsel

Existing CUA Law alumni in any of the categories above are particularly welcome.

## What the program asks of a mentor

A minimum of two meetings per semester with a paired student. Format and venue are flexible: coffee, video call, walk-and-talk, working session, whatever fits. The program runs the pairing process so the match fits your practice and the student's direction.

## To volunteer as a mentor

Email Alexander Hoffarth at hoffarth@cua.edu with a brief note:

1. Your name, firm or organization, and title
2. Your practice area and the kind of mentorship you can most usefully offer
3. The kind of student you would be most useful to (transactional, regulatory, entrepreneurial, in-house, fintech, public-interest, mission organization)
4. Any specific limit on availability (geographic, time, sector)

Mentorship slots are limited. The program is deliberately conservative about how many pairings are opened per year so each pairing can be substantive.

## To hire from the program

The first dedicated LEP cohort graduates in 2029. Until then, the program serves CUA Law students who self-identify with the LEP cohort. To make those students available for summer associate, postgraduate, or externship opportunities, contact the program directors.

The program is also building relationships with employers who hire from CUA Law more broadly. If you would like to be included in conversations about specific recruiting cycles, externship pipelines, or document-portfolio review, email the directors.

## To make a gift in support of the program

Direct giving conversations through Peggy Kacergis, Assistant Dean of Advancement at CUA Columbus School of Law, kacergis@cua.edu. For program-specific giving discussions, also copy the program directors.
