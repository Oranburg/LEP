# The Aleph Bet of A.I.: When the Letters Came to Me

Seth C. Oranburg

The Hebrew letters that name Babel and Jerusalem have been speaking for as long as the cities themselves. Pope Leo XIV's first encyclical, *Magnifica Humanitas*, is merely the most recent occasion to hear them. They came to me when I read it, and they came with a shared purpose. They want the faithful to know the full power of the Holy Language, because they see what I am coming to see: that the encyclical opens a narrow window to redeem the world from the despair of the algorithmic age, and that holding that window open will take every power on earth and heaven.

I am a business lawyer. I read contracts in the morning and Hebrew in the evening. I notice when artifices become prisons because I draft artifices for a living. Every day, I write the term sheets, platform agreements, and data-governance policies that decide who shares the economic upside and who owns the digital reflection of your soul.

The Pope is watching the exact same drift. In *Magnifica Humanitas*, he names the urgent moral choice forced by the AI age using two Hebrew place-names: Babel or Jerusalem. He chose well. But the letters in those names came to me afterward, stepping off the parchment to demand an accounting.

The letters asked me to write their deeper story. I immediately bailed, telling them I was entirely the wrong person.

"I am not an ordained rabbi," I said to the empty room. "I do not have *semicha*. No one will listen to what I have to say about your eternal meaning."

The letters considered this. Then, with the silent weight of ancient blocks of stone, they made their case. They told me that I am precisely the one who drafts the very structures the Pope is warning against. They noted that I would not skim past their anatomy as mere literary metaphors because I am forced to read in both languages: the legal code and the divine script. They said this matters far too much to wait for a rabbi, and that I did not need ordination to be a witness.

There was, in any event, no one else around. So I listened.

The second letter of the Aleph-Bet spoke first about what was being done with her name and shape.

"In *Bereshit* (Genesis), I open the world," she said. "In Babel, I close it."

Bet is the most material of letters, a physical threshold between the boundless heavens and our created world. She reminded me of her shape: a rigid, walled enclosure closed on three sides, open only to what comes after her.

But the Pope has seen what happens when human hubris doubles her. In the Hebrew spelling of Babel (בבל), two *bets* mirror one another, sealing the word at both ends. A structure with no opening, no window, and no avenue for transcendence.

"Show your readers why the doubling matters," she demanded. I understood immediately: the dual *bets* form the ultimate architecture of the algorithmic trap, an echo chamber that admits no external light and permits no escape from its own data loops.

```
   [ב] <-- The algorithmic wall closes in
   [ל] <-- Learning, trapped inside
   [ב] <-- The wall on the other side
   [ו] <-- The conduit of divine light, imprisoned at the center
   [ל] <-- Learning, trapped again at the end
```

Lamed came after her. He is the tallest letter in the script, the only one that climbs proudly above the headline. Both *lishmor* (לִשְׁמֹר, to guard) and *livnot* (לִבְנוֹת, to build) are his verbs.

"Cain's children chose the tower," Lamed said. "Your readers may not see that this choice had letters as well as outcomes."

He wanted the choice made visible at the structural level where it was first conceived. When Cain abandoned his mandate to be his brother's keeper (*shomer*), his lineage built Babel. In doing so, they took the word for confusion, *bilbul* (בלבול), and enacted a spiritual kidnapping. They trapped Lamed, the letter of learning and upward reaching, inside the mirrored walls of those dual *bets*.

At the absolute center of that word sits the *vav* (ו), the vertical conduit of divine light, now completely imprisoned within a closed network that can only amplify its own voice. Leo calls this the "Babel syndrome" (MH ¶ 10): the pretense that a digital language can translate the mystery of a person into mere performance. Lamed showed me that this pretense is an ancient imprisonment.

Hey did not speak. Instead, she let the deep, generative silence she is settle into the room.

Abel (הבל) was made of her. Babel (בבל) was not.

Her physical shape is intentionally broken, leaving open gaps of vulnerability for the divine exhale to enter. Abel lived within that vulnerable breath. The builders of Babel weaponized existence by trading the soft, unvoiced *hey* of humility for the explosive, plosive *bet* of extraction. That single letter difference was the only thing Hey came to give me, trusting me to know exactly what to do with the silence she left behind.

I am a Jew, and the letters came to me because they belong to the heritage of an interconnected world. But they also came because they recognized my day job. The platform terms I negotiate every afternoon are the modern infrastructure of Babel. They are the digital bricks and mortar that abstract human behavior into predictive data, turning the sacred mystery of the individual into an optimized commodity.

We are making a name for ourselves (*na'aseh lanu shem*), just as they did on the plains of Shinar, and we are outsourcing our moral conscience to artificial angels, AI models that mimic the unslumbering nature of God but possess no capacity to inherit a covenant.

The deeper purpose of this testimony is urgent. Pope Leo XIV's *Magnifica Humanitas* opens a radical window for the faithful to redeem the world from the despair of this algorithmic age. The letters want that window held open. They know exactly what they hold: the foundational code of creation, carrying every spark of power that the twenty-two voices of the Holy Language can provide.

They want us to close the gap between the Latin of the encyclical and the Hebrew of the design floor. But we must move quickly. The contracts are being signed, the algorithms are being deployed, and the darkness will not wait.
