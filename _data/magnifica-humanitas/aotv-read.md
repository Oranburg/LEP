# Core Argument (two sentences)

The entrepreneurial lawyer is not a transaction-cost engineer (contra Gilson) but a sub-creator: an architect of legal bodies — charters, operating agreements, governance structures — that give the entrepreneur's animating vision the institutional form it needs to act in the world, hire workers, raise capital, and generate self-sufficiency at commercial scale. This vocation, grounded in Maimonides's eighth degree of *tzedakah* (the gift that enables self-sufficiency), Aquinas's account of the *imago Dei* as intellect and will, and Tolkien's sub-creator framing, is distinctively human because it requires judgment under genuine Knightian uncertainty — the capacity AI can simulate but cannot exercise.

# Thesis Paragraph (verbatim, Introduction §IV)

> This Essay argues that the Entrepreneurial Lawyer exercises *phronesis*, the practical wisdom that Aristotle identified as the intellectual virtue governing action under conditions of uncertainty, and that this exercise constitutes participation in creation itself. The Entrepreneurial Lawyer is a Sub-Creator: a builder of normative orders where none existed, an architect of institutions that enable human flourishing. This understanding reframes both the vocation of business lawyering and the challenge that artificial intelligence poses to the legal profession. Current frontier models threaten to commoditize technical legal work. The construction of new normative orders in conditions of genuine uncertainty remains distinctively human, because it requires judgment, wisdom, and decision: capacities that AI can simulate but cannot exercise.

(AotV, Introduction, ¶ beginning "This Essay argues…")

# Magnifica Humanitas Convergences (four passages with line references)

**Convergence 1: The Babel/Jerusalem dichotomy.**
AotV's epigraph opens with Gen 1:1-2 ("tohu va-vohu") and the essay's body language returns to the void throughout. The founder "stares into the *tohu va-vohu*" (AotV, §VI, ¶ "John Paul II's *Laborem Exercens*…"). Leo XIV's ¶ 7 frames the encyclical between Babel as prideful concentration and Nehemiah as distributed rebuilding. AotV does not cite *Magnifica Humanitas* in its main body (the encyclical was issued May 15, 2026; AotV's last_revised date is May 20), but the body-soul language and the void/form contrast are structurally identical to the Babel/Nehemiah contrast the encyclical establishes.

**Convergence 2: Dignity of work in the AI transition.**
"The Entrepreneurial Lawyer's work is dignified not because it produces legal documents (AI can produce legal documents) but because it requires the exercise of practical wisdom, the application of judgment to particular circumstances, the cultivation of virtue through the habitual practice of discernment." (AotV, §XI "Beneficial Constraints and the Dignity of Work," ¶ beginning "The documents are the objective product…") — This is the precise claim *Magnifica Humanitas* makes about the subjective dimension of work surviving AI automation. The encyclical's concern that AI "speed and ease" extinguishes the desire to ask questions (§140, cited in the havruta insertion) is the encyclical's curricular corollary to AotV's curricular proposal in §XV.

**Convergence 3: Institution as carrier of human flourishing / subsidiarity.**
"The entrepreneurial firm embodies subsidiarity. It is a form of private ordering in which individuals voluntarily associate to accomplish what they could not accomplish alone, without recourse to the state or to larger institutions." (AotV, §V, ¶ beginning "The entrepreneurial firm embodies subsidiarity.") — This maps to *Magnifica Humanitas* ¶ 71 (subsidiarity requires transparency, accountability, and meaningful participation) and to the *magnifica-jewish-asymmetry.md* draft's development of *kehilla* governance as the proof of concept for subsidiarity at scale.

**Convergence 4: Limits on commodification.**
"Does this venture, as structured, tend toward enabling self-sufficiency and human agency? Or does it tend toward dependency, extraction, and the degradation of dignity?" (AotV, §XIV, ¶ beginning "This is why the Entrepreneurial Lawyer needs not merely technical skill…") — This diagnostic question is AotV's practical translation of Bridge 6. *Magnifica Humanitas* ¶¶ 173-174 (hidden labor chains, new forms of slavery) and ¶ 178 (data colonialism) are the encyclical's named instances of the same degradation. The havruta insertion explicitly cites *Magnifica Humanitas* §140 for the AI-as-shortcut-versus-AI-as-thinking-partner distinction.
