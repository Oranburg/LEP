# Judgment Proof: Read for *Magnifica Humanitas* Engagement

## Core Argument (two sentences)

Modern regulatory and compliance architecture has eliminated the human judgment it was built to depend upon, producing institutions that are "judgment-proof" in both the legal sense (no assets from which a verdict can be collected) and the epistemic sense (no faculty of discernment against which accountability could operate). The book argues that the Western legal tradition, including Maimonides's *tikkun ha-guf* / *tikkun ha-nefesh* distinction and Aquinas's account of law's two purposes, understood this failure mode before the seventeenth-century retreat that replaced law's "scaffolding" function with law's exclusive "foundation" function — and that recovery requires designing friction back into institutional architecture, not eliminating regulation.

## Direct *Magnifica Humanitas* Connections

**Bridge 4 (institution as carrier of flourishing) is the spine of JP.**

*Magnifica Humanitas* Chapter 4 on work in the AI age treats AI-driven displacement of human judgment as a problem of institutional design, not merely an economic disruption. JP supplies the doctrinal vocabulary for that problem: the "Deskilling Loop" (the mechanism by which institutions replace judgment with rules, thereby destroying the capacity for the judgment the next generation of rules will also fail to supply); the Aristotelian *technê* / *phronêsis* distinction (what can be systematized versus what can only be exercised); Maimonides's *tikkun ha-nefesh* (law's second purpose, which cannot be achieved by coercion or rule, only by cultivation); and Aquinas's *determinatio* (the space left by general principles for particular judgment).

The *Magnifica Humanitas* engagement should use JP's vocabulary precisely: AI automates *technê* at scale, but the question the encyclical poses is what happens to *phronêsis* — the practical wisdom that is only developed through its own exercise — when the institutional architecture rewards its elimination. JP supplies the causal mechanism (the Deskilling Loop) and the historical genealogy (the seventeenth-century retreat from law's second purpose) that the encyclical raises but does not trace.

**Bridge 2 (property) enters via JP's treatment of the compliance industrial complex** as an institutional structure that renders certain actors effectively immune to accountability — the juridical analog to the property-without-social-function critique in *Rerum Novarum* and *Centesimus Annus*.

**Bridge 6 (limits on commodification) enters via JP's argument** that judgment itself is not a commodity. When Boeing converted the pilot's *phronêsis* into MCAS software, it commodified what the tradition says cannot be commodified without destroying it.

## Strongest Single Move for *Magnifica Humanitas*

The "double meaning" frame from Chapter 1 is the move the LEP engagement should lift directly. JP argues that institutions become judgment-proof in the liability sense *precisely because* they have become judgment-proof in the epistemic sense: eliminate judgment, eliminate accountability for the wisdom that was never applied. Applied to the AI-age question, the move reads: AI systems that displace human judgment in law, medicine, and governance do not merely make those systems more efficient; they make them immune to accountability in both senses, because no human judgment was ever exercised and no human can be held responsible for the discernment that was never made. *Magnifica Humanitas* §§ 241-242 (Nehemiah / the wall) supplies the counter-image: the work of rebuilding requires *each family assigned its own section*, the irreducible particularity of individual responsibility that the Deskilling Loop destroys.

## JP–LE Relation

JP operates at the institutional-design layer (what happens when institutions eliminate judgment) and LE operates at the anthropological-philological layer (what the grammar of work tells us about the human person as sustained exerciser of a venture). They converge on the same AI-age problem from opposite directions: JP traces the destruction of judgment through the institutional architecture; LE traces the theological and legal anthropology of the person as *exercens* who can only constitute herself through the activity she exercises. Together they supply a diagnosis (JP) and a norm (LE) for the LEP fellowship argument.
