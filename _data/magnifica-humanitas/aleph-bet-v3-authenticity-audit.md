## Drift Detected

### 1. Integralist-defusal expansion (v3 line 35): not in v1, imported from v2

v1 ends the bilbul paragraph cleanly: "Hebrew shows why that pretense fails at the level of the letter."

v3 inserts between that sentence and the Augustine citation:

> "The Babel syndrome is not the danger of shared conviction but of algorithmic reduction; what is forbidden is not unity but the pretense that one technical grammar can exhaust the particular person."

This sentence was not Seth's. It came from the v2 pass (where it read "totalizing reduction"). v3 swapped "totalizing" for "algorithmic" and kept the rest. The syntax — doubled "not X but Y; what is forbidden is not X but Y" — is not Seth's register. Seth does not write paired antitheses stacked in a semicolon-linked sentence. The sentence also violates the no-negation-framing rule twice.

**Disposition:** This sentence should either be dropped (the paragraph works without it — the Augustine citation follows the bilbul analysis directly) or Seth should rewrite it as a positive claim in his own voice.

### 2. "God" vs. "G-d" slip (v3 line 51)

Every other use of the divine name in v3 follows the Jewish orthographic convention ("G-d"). The second Aquinas parenthetical reads: "Aquinas calls the interior life something the human holds in trust from God (ST II-II Q.64 a.1)." One "God" in a document of thirty-plus "G-d"s is an editorial fingerprint, not Seth's choice. The inconsistency is either a slip or the editor defaulting to standard usage inside what felt like a technical citation. Either way, it needs normalizing.

### 3. Kenosis sentence register (v3 line 17): authorized but not rephrased into Seth's voice

v1: "By contracting himself through a mystical process known as tzimtzim, G-d obscured His light in vessels so humans can exist in spaces not entirely filled by His divine presence."

v3 adds the authorized kenosis insert: "Catholic readers will recognize a structural parallel in the doctrine of kenosis (Philippians 2:7), the self-emptying by which the divine makes room for the human."

The content is correct and authorized. But the phrasing "the self-emptying by which the divine makes room for the human" is voice-neutral theological shorthand — it scans as editor prose, not Seth. Seth's natural move at this kind of parallel is declarative and concrete. Compare his own bilbul sentence structure — he names the mechanism and lets it speak. A phrase like "the same contraction, enacted in flesh" or even just the citation with a plain gloss would sit in his register. As written it is harmless but reads imported.

## What the v3 Pass Got Right

**Lamed paragraph.** v1 had "Hebrew's longest letter" (philologically imprecise) and "Indeed," an AI tell. v3 has "Hebrew's tallest letter, the only one that rises above the script's headline" (precise, visual, Seth-appropriate) and drops "Indeed" cleanly. The sentence shape is preserved; the precision is improved. This is exactly what surgical editing should do.

**Shomer paragraph.** v1 had em dashes inside the vav clause; v3 converts to commas. The sentence is word-for-word otherwise. Preserved intact.

**Cain parenthetical.** v1: "Ka'Yin, קין – means 'acquisition.'" v3: "Kayin, קין, means 'acquisition.'" The transliteration is normalized; the punctuation is cleaned; the content and rhythm are Seth's. The addition "two of the letters whose strokes descend below the baseline" replaces "lowest-reaching letters in the Hebrew alphabet" with a more precise anatomical description. Seth would own this.

**"Moral substrate" removed.** v2 added "That covenantal capacity, the moral substrate of every genuine promise." v3 drops the apposition and restores v1's clean line: "That covenantal capacity is what lishmor requires." Correctly excised.

**Italics removed.** v2 italicized every Hebrew term in the body (*shoresh*, *lamed*, *shomer*, *bilbul*, *malakh*, etc.). v3 returns to Seth's plain-text style. The italics were a house-style choice that was not Seth's and would need a venue decision anyway.

**New lede (v3 line 5).** The v1 lede announced the essay's argument in flat summary ("This Essay illuminates the Jewish geneology of these illustrations"). The v3 replacement opens in medias res: "In Magnifica Humanitas, Pope Leo XIV frames the AI age as a choice between two cities, Babel and Jerusalem. He chose his images well, and a Catholic reader who hears them in their original Hebrew finds a layer of teaching the translation cannot fully carry." This lede is better than v1 and reads as Seth's register — declarative, economical, the second sentence pulling the reader forward without overpromising. Whether Seth authorized this lede rewrite or whether it carried over from v2 is unclear from the files, but it does not read as an overreach. It sounds like Seth on a good day.

**Augustine sentence (v3 line 35, end).** "Augustine reads Babel's dispersion as providential refusal of human self-glorification (De Civitate Dei XVI.4)." Short, declarative, citation anchored. This is neutral enough that the voice question does not arise. Sits cleanly.

**Subsidiarity sentence (v3 line 43).** "Nehemiah rebuilt the wall by assigning each family its own section (Nehemiah 3): subsidiarity in built form." Five words after the colon do the work of the five-line v2 gloss. This is Seth's compression instinct. Well done.

**Aquinas imago Dei (v3 line 47).** "Aquinas calls this capacity the imago Dei (ST I Q.93 a.1)." One sentence, appended to Seth's own argument, citation anchored. Does not redirect the prose; it confirms. Fine.

**NT resonance parenthetical.** "Psalm 118:22, taken up in Matthew 21:42, Mark 12:10, and 1 Peter 2:7." Citation list, no new prose. Does not introduce voice. Fine.
