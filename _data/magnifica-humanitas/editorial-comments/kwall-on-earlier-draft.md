The Aleph Bet of A.I.
Seth C. Oranburg
Headline Catholic news celebrates Leo XIV’s new encyclical contrasting the “Babel” constructed by AI run amok with the “Jerusalem” built by humans in partnership. But while Magnifica Humanitas is ostensibly about new things (rerum novarum), the Biblical Hebrew illuminates the Holy See’s eternal lessons about the enduring nature of this world.  I am  having a hard time with this open.  It doesn’t draw me in or even give me a sense of what your take is going to be. It is also a little hard to get through from my perspective.  I also think it is a bit obtuse—especially for someone with no background ( I would include me here).  In short, you need to lay better groundwork to give the reader more background and preview your take.
To begin, let’s assume that Hebrew is a Holy language of immense power, for G-d literally “said” all that exists into being ex nihilo using the 22 Hebrew letters. Gen. 1:3 et sec. Moreover, each Hebrew letter (and thus each word) has a numerical equivalent. From this perspective, Hebrew words are not mere symbols, they are the Platonic essence of the thing itself, which we can deeply understand through philology and gematria, the mathematical study of these letters and their combinations.  I word start by saying something more like: According to Jewish tradition, God spoke the world into existence.  The Torah states: “XXXXX” Also, in popular writing you should spell out G-d even though that feels strange to those of us more observant. Continuing on, you are writing at a  pretty high level of specific knowledge.  You need to “bring this down” to a normal person’s level.  Do most people understand “the Platonic essence?”  I’m not even sure I do!!!
Then let’s place the story of Babel in its Biblical context. G-d completed his creation. Now it’s Adam and Eve’s turn to sub-create. They produce children; Eve names the second one Abel (הבל). The Hebrew here is revealing, as Abel is just one (first) letter different from Babel (בבל). Where is all this going?  What is the general point?  You need to help the reader more.
What is the meaning of the name Abel? Bible students may recall Abel “kept flocks.” Gen. 4:2. This occupation is gentle and fleeting, it does not modify the earth, and his name reflects this in its meaning of “vapor” or “smoke.” Indeed, Abel pleases G-d by offering Him a burnt sacrifice. Abel’s transitory smoke naturally ascends, then disappears, acknowledging G-d’s sovereignty.
Lamed (ל) is the tallest letter in the Hebrew alphabet. It signifies learning as a reaching up to the divine source of all Truth. You might imagine the smoke from Abel’s animal sacrifice curling up to G-d like the lamed.  Again, who is your audience?  What does this have to do with the Pope?
But, as careful readers know, it is not Abel’s progeny but Cain’s children that build Babel. Who is Cain? His name (קין) means “acquisition.” He farmed and thus took ownership of the land. And his very name includes the lowest letters in the Hebrew alphabet. Indeed, both the first letter, kof (ק), and last letter, the nun sofit (ן), of Cain’s name reach down to grab onto the roots of the earth.  Will most people care about this????
Here is what happens when the instinct to reach up to heaven is married to the desire to acquire and possess durably.  Again, the writing style is hard to push through.  The children of Cain set about building a great tower that would permanently connect them to heaven. OK- this last sentence is clear.  Maybe this is your starting point.   And this tower is named appropriately. It shares Abel’s lamed (ל), again, who are your readers? Will they all know what a lamed is? as both stretch toward heaven, but it replaces the hey (ה) of Abel’s humility (phonetically, hey is the least assertive sound in language, an unvoiced exhale, nothing more than a breath; and its shape is broken, leaving open gaps for divine energy to enter) with the bet (ב) of fortification (a “plosive” consonant that traps and forcefully bursts air outward, this letter’s shape is rigid and open only on one side).  Same question—I’ll be honest here—I am a solid middle level Hebrew speaker who clearly knows the alphabet.  I’m struggling with this.
Moreover, Babel (בבל) has two bet’s. Imagine them as mirroring one another, and you get a structure with no opening at all. Babel is, in truth, an echo chamber. This needs much more explanation and previewing.
This is the deeper meaning of Pope Leo’s warning about artificial intelligence: it can be a trap. While it may look like a durable path to heaven, there are no such routes in this material world. Instead, the risk is a purely algorithmic world made entirely of paired bet’s” sealed, hyper-constructed, artificial homes that lock humanity inside.   Start with this and make your argument more clear and direct.  By this point, I’m lost.  Also, you are assuming too much background knowledge of your reader at the top—you need much more conteoxt.
The result of this babel will undoubtably be confusion, which, in Hebrew, is bilbul (בלבול). The danger symbolized by the stacked bet’s is aggravated by the locked lamed’s. Trapped inside is the lonely vav (ו), whose job (symbolized by its appearance as a straight up and down line) to bring Divine light into the human world is literally trapped and burned deep inside the repetitive human loop of learning from oneself.
It seems to me that Pope Leo’s encyclical reminds us to retain the humility and openness of hey (ה) so our souls do not become trapped in the very machinery of bet (ב) that we artifice.
