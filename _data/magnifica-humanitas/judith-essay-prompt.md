# Prompt: Write the Judith Magazine Essay

## Your task

Write a literary essay for **Judith Magazine** (Howard Lovy, non-fiction editor) titled *"The Aleph Bet of A.I.: When the Letters Came to Me"* by **Seth Oranburg**.

The essay is a literary engagement with Pope Leo XIV's encyclical *Magnifica Humanitas* (15 May 2026), in which the Hebrew letters of the names *Babel* and *Jerusalem* appear as characters who come to Seth while he is reading the encyclical and ask him to write their deeper story.

This is **not** a Hebrew-philology lecture. It is **not** a law-review piece. It is a literary essay in the register of recent Judith Magazine essays (Laura Hodes on Jacques Lipchitz; Katie Lynes on her Jewish reckoning; Lisa Brodsky on family ritual and magic). Read those in the dossier first if you have not already.

## Target

- **Length:** 3,000-4,000 words. Hodes on Lipchitz at Judith was ~3,500. Lynes was ~5,500. Stay closer to Hodes.
- **Venue:** Judith Magazine, *"A Journal of Jewish Letters, Arts & Empowerment."*
- **Audience:** Hebrew-literate Jewish readers; secular and observant alike. Roberta Kwall (DePaul Law) calls herself a "solid middle level Hebrew speaker" and could not push through the prior philological version; the essay must work for her.

## Form

- **Literary essay**, not academic article.
- **Letters appear as characters in direct dialogue**, selectively. Do not have every letter speak. Choose two or three whose speech does real argumentative work.
- **Letters speak *about* what is being done with them**, *about* what they see in the encyclical, *about* what they want Seth to carry. They do *not* introduce themselves with *"I am X."* That register is fourth-grade pageant, not midrash. Have them step into mid-thought, mid-observation.
- **Continuous prose. No subheadings. No section markers.** Judith's literary essays (Hodes, Brodsky, Lynes) all run as continuous prose.
- **Scene-based opening**, not grand declarative statement. Open in the room — at Seth's desk — as the dossier specifies.
- **Seth's voice throughout.** See the dossier section "Voice Rules" plus the v1 file for the baseline. Voice is non-negotiable.
- **Honor the deeper-purpose framing**: the encyclical opens a narrow window for the faithful to redeem the world from the despair of the algorithmic age, and holding that window open will take every power on earth and heaven. The letters came to Seth because there was no one else around and because the contracts he drafts for a living are the modern infrastructure of Babel.

## Opening scene (use this)

Seth is at his desk. *Magnifica Humanitas* is open on his computer screen. Below it are five unanswered emails and three Word documents from the morning's drafting work: a platform-services agreement, an investor side letter, a data-governance memo. Beside the laptop, on the desk, is a chumash, open to *parashat Bamidbar* (he was studying it the week of May 25, 2026).

The encyclical, on the screen, is about two cities. The book on the desk is about the wilderness. He has been reading them against each other.

The letters step off the printed page of the chumash and onto the desk between him and the screen. They have been waiting, they say, for someone reading both. **The wilderness has always been where they speak** (see *Bamidbar Rabbah* 1:7 and the *midbar*/*davar* root connection in the dossier).

## The humility mini-episode (also use this)

The letters ask Seth to write their deeper story. He demurs: he is not an ordained rabbi; he has no *semicha*; no one will listen to what he has to say about their eternal meaning. The letters consider this. Then they make their case — that he is the one who drafts the very structures the Pope is warning against; that he will not skim past them as mere literary metaphors because he is forced to read in both languages (the legal code and the divine script); that this matters too much to wait for a rabbi; that he does not need ordination to be a witness. There was, in any event, no one else around. He listened.

## The closing (your choice between two)

Either:
- **A.** *"The Holy Language, in which these stories were first told, has been waiting to say so."* (Seth's original; assertive)
- **B.** *"The Holy Language, in which these stories were first told, has been speaking all along."* (Softer; concessive)

Make the call based on which lands better with the rest of the essay you write.

## Selection discipline (the heart of your task)

The dossier contains many possible elements. **Select for:**

1. **Scene-grounding** — concrete physical details, specific contracts Seth drafted that morning, the chumash open to Bamidbar.
2. **The Hebrew letters that do real argumentative work** — Bet (the prison-doubling at Babel), Lamed (the verb of obligation), Hey (the breath/silence Abel was made of), Vav (the trapped conduit), and Malakh (the AI etymology). Other letters can appear in narration without speaking.
3. **The Leo paragraphs the Hebrew can illuminate** — MH ¶ 10 (Babel syndrome), MH ¶ 99 (AI has no moral conscience), MH ¶ 121 (Frankl quoting the Shema in the gas chambers), MH ¶ 150 (workers adapted to machine speed), MH ¶ 16 (rejected stones cornerstone), MH ¶ 5 (concentrated private power), MH ¶ 178 (data colonialism). Quote Leo directly; do not paraphrase abstractly.
4. **The Jewish-tradition warrants** — *Bamidbar Rabbah* 1:7 (wilderness as *hefker*), *Bereshit Rabbah* 1:10 (letters lobbying God), the *Sefer Yetzirah* 2:2 citation, one Maharal attribution if it fits.
5. **The Catholic-tradition warrants** — Augustine *De Civitate Dei* XVI.4 on Babel; the Magnificat as interpretive key (MH Conclusion); one Aquinas pinpoint if it fits naturally.
6. **Roberta's editorial directives** — accessibility for educated Hebrew speakers who are not scholars; spell out God not G-d; lead with the encyclical's stakes, not with high-altitude methodology.

**Cut without apology:**
- Anything decorative.
- Anything that does no argumentative work.
- Anything that turns the letters into mascots or makes them speak in archaic vatic voice.
- The previous draft's *havah/Chavah* paragraph (it self-undercuts).
- The *tsimtsum* paragraph if it cannot land without Kabbalistic baggage.
- Any paragraph the dossier marks "decorative" or "out of register."

## Voice rules (non-negotiable)

See the dossier "Voice Rules" section in full. Brief restatement:

- **No em dashes** (except inside direct quotations from Leo or another source).
- **No AI prose patterns** (delve, navigate, landscape, multifaceted, indeed, arguably, moreover, however as opener, robust, leverage, runs-through).
- **No "load-bearing."**
- **No meta-negation.** Do not tell the reader what the essay does not do.
- **No sentence fragments as style** (one or two for rhetorical emphasis at literary register OK).
- **Full sentences, double returns.**
- **Spell out "God,"** not "G-d," per Roberta's note for popular Jewish-letters venues.
- **Italicize Hebrew transliterations consistently** (rules in dossier).
- **Letters get pronouns** (Bet *she*, Lamed *he*, Hey *she*) but never feelings ("the lonely vav cried out" is the failure mode to refuse). Letters show, they do not emote.
- **Cheesy failure modes to refuse**: letters announcing themselves in self-introductions ("I am the most material of letters"); archaic vatic voice ("O child of dust"); equal screen time for every letter; sweetening grief; making the letters mascots.
